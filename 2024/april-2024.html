News from the Code Enigma Team
April already, and we're sending our first newsletter of 2024. Time flies when you're busy looking after some of Europe's biggest Drupal websites! As usual, we have a few important pieces of information to share:

Nextcloud Update

We have moved our file sharing service, Nextcloud, to a brand new server. Any files you had previously saved on there are still available on the old server, at https://nextcloud-archive.codeenigma.net. We'll keep this running until 11th July 2024, so if you need anything on it please grab it before then.

Code Enigma Away Week

As most of you probably know, every year we try to have an annual conference so all the staff can get together somewhere nice to talk about business, tech and just spend some face time together. This year it's going to be a Friday to Friday affair, from 28th June to the 5th July. For those six days our service will be limited to support only.

As ever, the usual alert systems will go out to all relevant staff should an issue arise.

Meet Sunil, our new Cloud Operations man
Continuing on the theme of people, allow us to introduce Sunil, who is posing here on an appropriately cloudy background. Many of you have probably come across him already, he's been a member of the systems team for over a year now. However, what you may not realise is since the start of 2024 he has a dedicated new role. Sunil is heading up Cloud Operations, which is a new thing for Code Enigma.

What does that mean for you? Well, simply put, Sunil is now charged with reviewing all our cloud usage and looking for opportunities to optimise, explore new technology, save money, enhance security, and so on. He has a wide remit within the scope of Cloud services such as AWS and Azure and he is now dedicated to getting the most out of our cloud suppliers, for Code Enigma and for you, our clients.

Feel free to reach out to him via our ticketing system if you have questions about your cloud services, and you can expect to hear from him with advice and suggestions going forwards.

Official LocalGov Drupal Hosting Partners
We're delighted to announce we have signed up as hosting partners for the outstanding LocalGov Drupal project. You can also find us at LocalGov Drupal Camp in Birmingham, so feel free to approach Phil or Sunil if you have any questions for us.

We're also delighted to report we are helping a new(ish) client, the Royal Borough of Greenwich, with their roll-out of LocalGov Drupal, having been introduced to them via our friends at AWS where we are Public Sector Partners. They are moving their sites one at a time, and recently launched their first LocalGov Drupal site, their revamped local community directory.

Important software updates
There are two important software updates on our radar in the first half of 2024. MySQL 5.7 has been End Of Life on most platforms since October 2023, although AWS provided longer support on their RDS platform. They are still providing long term support, however since 1st March 2024 there are extra charges for it. If your applications still operate MySQL 5.7 you should upgrade to MySQL 8.0 as soon as possible to be secure and to save money.

Debian 10 is also coming up to end of life, and with it goes our legacy configuration management system, Puppet. Between now and the end of June all servers will be upgraded to Debian 11 and will be Ansible managed. You should not be affected by this, but if there is any build disruption that is likely the cause.

Containerisation is here!
Our modern, Ansible-based deploy tool, ce-deploy, now supports containerisation of your applications. The deploy_container role can handle a number of different scenarios, from simply packing and pushing a Docker container to a repository of your choice to rolling out an AWS Elastic Container Service (ECS) "task" and launching it using the AWS Fargate serverless container platform. We expect our first client-built service to launch using this technology very soon, it will be a NextJS search app with an Apache Solr back end.

We have no immediate plans for Kubernetes support, as the combination of AWS Elastic Container Registry, ECS and Fargate currently provide the simplest and best supported means, in the AWS product landscape, of publishing containerised and serverless applications.
